# Associate Software Engineer

**Level:** IC1 (Associate)

## Role Overview

The Associate Software Engineer is an early-career contributor focused on learning fundamentals and building a foundation for long-term growth. This role emphasizes developing core technical skills, understanding team practices, and contributing reliably to well-defined tasks with appropriate guidance and support.

Success in this role is defined by consistent improvement, not perfection. Associate Engineers are expected to ask thoughtful questions, incorporate feedback effectively, and demonstrate growing competence over time. With structured guidance from more senior team members, they build the skills and judgment needed to take on increasingly independent work.

## Success Outcomes

Success in this role means achieving the following outcomes:

### Learning and Growth

- Foundational skills that are actively developing through structured guidance, practice, and feedback
- Growing competency with the team's core tools, technologies, and development workflows
- An understanding of team processes and how work gets planned, reviewed, and delivered
- Consistent improvement in both technical skills and professional judgment over time

### Reliable Execution

- Clearly defined tasks completed with appropriate quality and within expected timeframes
- Work that meets basic quality standards and team conventions, improving with each review cycle
- Proactive communication about progress, blockers, and when help is needed
- Established patterns and practices followed consistently, with growing understanding of the reasoning behind them

### Effective Learning Behaviors

- Thoughtful questions asked that demonstrate engagement, effort, and a desire to understand
- Feedback incorporated effectively, with visible growth from one review cycle to the next
- Self-awareness about knowledge gaps and appropriate judgment about when to ask for help
- Notes, documentation, and learning artifacts that support personal growth and reduce repeat questions

### Team Collaboration

- Positive contributions to team culture through curiosity, humility, and consistent effort
- Working relationships with immediate team members built on reliability and good communication
- Active participation in team meetings and ceremonies, even when still learning the context
- A willingness to help with team tasks and contribute where possible, within current skill level

## Required Skills

### Technical Foundation

- Basic knowledge of programming fundamentals: variables, control flow, data structures, and functions
- Familiarity with at least one programming language used in professional development
- Basic understanding of version control concepts (Git) and collaborative development workflows
- Awareness of testing concepts and why quality assurance matters
- Ability to learn new tools and technologies with guidance

### Professional Skills

- Clear written and verbal communication about work status and needs
- Time management and ability to meet commitments with appropriate support
- Receptiveness to feedback and coaching
- Basic problem-solving within defined parameters

### Core Competencies

- Curiosity and genuine interest in software development
- Persistence in working through challenges with appropriate support
- Humility to acknowledge what you don't know and ask for help
- Attention to detail and care for quality in work product

## Required Experience

### Years of Experience

- 0-2 years of professional software development experience

### Background

- Recent graduate, bootcamp completer, or career changer with foundational technical training
- Completion of relevant coursework, projects, or self-directed learning demonstrating technical aptitude
- Portfolio of work (academic projects, personal projects, bootcamp capstones, or open source contributions)

### Technical Exposure

- Experience writing code in at least one programming language, in any context
- Exposure to collaborative development (group projects, pair programming, or team-based work)
- Basic familiarity with development environments, debugging, and troubleshooting

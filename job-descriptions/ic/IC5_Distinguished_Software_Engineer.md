# Distinguished Software Engineer

**Level:** IC5 (Distinguished)

## Role Overview

The Distinguished Software Engineer operates at the highest level of individual technical contribution, with impact that extends across the organization and into the broader industry. This role shapes technical vision at the company level, builds lasting competitive advantages through technical excellence, and defines what "world-class" looks like in our engineering organization.

Success in this role requires rare depth and breadth of expertise, the ability to anticipate industry shifts and position the company accordingly, and a track record of solving previously unsolved problems. Distinguished Engineers are recognized thought leaders both internally and externally, and their influence extends far beyond their direct involvement through the systems, practices, and people they develop.

## Success Outcomes

Success in this role means achieving the following outcomes:

### Company-Level Technical Strategy

- Technical vision and strategy that shapes the company's direction and creates lasting competitive advantage
- Decisions with company-level implications made with sound judgment—balancing technical excellence, business needs, and organizational realities
- Standards for technical excellence defined and embodied that establish what "world-class" means in our organization
- Critical risks and opportunities identified before they become obvious, with proactive strategies to address them

### Transformative Impact

- Previously unsolved problems solved with novel approaches that unlock new capabilities or markets
- Systems, platforms, or practices built that fundamentally change how the organization operates—creating step-function improvements
- Technical capabilities created that become core to the company's value proposition and competitive position
- Initiatives driven that significantly improve organizational efficiency, quality, or capability at scale

### Building the Next Generation

- Staff engineers (IC4s) developed into future Distinguished engineers and technical leaders
- Career paths and opportunities created for senior technical talent that enable long-term retention and growth
- Technical culture and community built at scale that attracts and develops exceptional engineers
- Practices, frameworks, and institutions established that outlast direct involvement and continue creating value

### Industry Leadership

- Recognition as a thought leader in the industry, with influence on how the broader community approaches problems
- Significant contributions to the external technical community through open source, research, publications, or conference leadership
- Talent and partnerships attracted through external reputation and visibility
- The company's technical brand strengthened through external representation and thought leadership

## Required Skills

### Technical Expertise

- Industry-recognized expertise with rare depth and breadth—among the best in the field
- Ability to see around corners—anticipating future challenges, technology shifts, and opportunities
- Novel problem-solving—creating original solutions to problems others cannot solve
- Deep understanding of the competitive landscape and what's technically possible vs. aspirational
- Architectural vision that spans the full technology stack and organizational context

### Executive Leadership

- Influence at C-level and board level on technical strategy and direction
- Strategic communication—framing technical vision in business terms for executive audiences
- Organizational design thinking—understanding how technical and organizational structures interact
- Change leadership—driving large-scale technical and cultural transformation

### Core Competencies

- Multiplier mindset—creating systems and developing people that scale impact beyond personal capacity
- Long-term thinking—making decisions that optimize for years, not quarters
- Teaching at scale—developing other senior technical leaders and shaping how engineering is practiced
- External presence—representing the company's technical excellence to the broader community

## Required Experience

### Years of Experience

- 12-20+ years of professional software development experience

### Demonstrated Track Record

- Multiple significant organizational-level impacts—a pattern of transformative contribution, not isolated successes
- Recognition as a technical leader both within and outside the company
- History of developing other Staff+ engineers who became technical leaders themselves
- Proven ability to drive company-level strategic initiatives from conception through execution
- Track record of solving novel, previously unsolved problems with original approaches

### Technical Experience

- Expertise across major systems and architectural patterns at scale
- Experience across different organizational contexts, company stages, or technical domains
- Deep understanding of how technical strategy connects to business strategy and competitive dynamics

### Industry Recognition

- Demonstrated thought leadership through publications, patents, conference keynotes, or major open source contributions
- Recognition by peers in the industry as an expert in the domain

### Education

- Bachelor's degree in Computer Science, Engineering, or related field—or equivalent practical experience. Advanced degrees valued but not required.

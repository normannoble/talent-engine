## ğŸ¯ Philosophy

### Outcome-Based Success Definition

Job descriptions define success through **outcomes** (desired end states), not **activities** (task lists):

**Instead of:** "Manage the product roadmap"  
**We write:** "A product roadmap that is clearly prioritized, aligned with company strategy, and understood by stakeholders"

**Instead of:** "Lead engineering team"  
**We write:** "A high-performing engineering team that consistently delivers quality work and meets commitments"

### Key Principles

1. **Outcomes over tasks** â€“ Describe what success looks like, not what activities to perform
2. **Level-appropriate expectations** â€“ Same outcome may be IC3 for one person, IC4 for another
3. **Cumulative competencies** â€“ Higher levels build on lower level foundations
4. **Multiple valid paths** â€“ Plateauing at IC3 or M2 represents strong career success
5. **Promotion = sustained performance** â€“ Must demonstrate next-level work before promotion

## ğŸ’¡ How to Use This Framework

### For Employees

- **Understand your role**: Read your job description in `job-descriptions/[track]/`
- **Plan your growth**: Review progression guides in `career-development/progression-guides/`
- **Assess promotion readiness**: Use assessments in `career-development/readiness-assessments/`
- **See the big picture**: Read career overviews in `career-development/overviews/`

### For Managers

- **Set expectations**: Use JDs to clarify role expectations with team members
- **Guide development**: Use progression guides for 1:1 conversations about growth
- **Evaluate readiness**: Use readiness assessments for promotion discussions
- **Conduct reviews**: Use assessment templates in `performance-management/templates/` for structured evaluations
- **Maintain consistency**: Reference framework during performance reviews and calibration

### For HR/People Ops

- **Hire accurately**: Use JDs to write job ads and evaluate candidates
- **Level consistently**: Apply framework criteria across hiring and promotions
- **Develop people**: Use progression guides in career development programs
- **Run performance cycles**: Use the performance management philosophy and templates
- **Extend the framework**: Use templates to create JDs for new roles or functions

### For Organizations Adopting This Framework

1. Review both framework approaches (Composable vs Traditional)
2. Choose which approach fits your organizational needs
3. Customize engineering examples for your tech stack if needed
4. Map existing roles to framework levels
5. Train managers on the framework and its application
6. Integrate into performance management and promotion processes
7. Expand to other functions (Product, Design, etc.) as needed

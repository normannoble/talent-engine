# 30-60-90 Day Onboarding: Head of Engineering (M3)

## Overview

**Level:** M3 — Head of Engineering
**Focus:** Understanding org capability, building leader relationships, shaping org direction
**Manager's Role:** Executive partner, organizational context, strategic alignment

M3s manage managers and shape organizational capability. The goal is to quickly assess organizational health and begin building leadership culture.

---

## Days 1-30: Learn

### Theme: Organizational Assessment

**Primary Objectives:**
- Assess the management team and organizational capability
- Build relationships with direct reports (managers) and skip-levels
- Understand organizational health, delivery patterns, and challenges
- Map stakeholder landscape and executive relationships

### Week 1: Leadership Orientation

| Day | Focus | Activities |
|-----|-------|------------|
| 1 | Welcome | Executive intro, org strategy, priorities |
| 2 | Leaders | 1:1s with each direct report (managers) |
| 3 | Organization | Org structure, team health, delivery patterns |
| 4 | Strategy | Technical strategy, roadmap, major initiatives |
| 5 | Executives | Meet product, design, and business leadership |

**Key Meetings to Schedule:**
- [ ] 1:1 with your manager (weekly)
- [ ] Extended 1:1 with each direct report manager
- [ ] Skip-level meetings with ICs (sample across org)
- [ ] Product leadership
- [ ] Peer executives (other Heads/Directors)
- [ ] Key stakeholders
- [ ] Previous leader (if available)

### Weeks 2-4: Deep Organizational Understanding

**Learning Goals:**
- [ ] Clear assessment of each manager's effectiveness
- [ ] View of organizational health across all teams
- [ ] Understanding of delivery patterns and capability
- [ ] Map of talent—strong performers, risks, potential
- [ ] Identified organizational improvement priorities

**Activities:**
- Leadership assessment for each manager
- Skip-level 1:1s across the organization
- Review team health and engagement data
- Understand technical strategy and execution
- Document organizational assessment

**Assessment Milestones:**
- [ ] Manager effectiveness profiles complete
- [ ] Organizational health assessment done
- [ ] Talent map created
- [ ] Key challenges identified
- [ ] Executive relationships forming

### 30-Day Check-in

**Your Manager Discussion Points:**
- What's your assessment of the management team?
- What's your view of organizational health?
- Where are the biggest capability gaps?
- What are your priorities for the next 60 days?
- What executive support do you need?

**Success Indicators:**
- [ ] Comprehensive organizational assessment complete
- [ ] Manager relationships established
- [ ] Skip-level connections made
- [ ] Executive relationships forming
- [ ] Clear on priorities and approach

- Drive cross-team alignment

**Developing Leaders:**

- [ ] Leadership rhythm effective

## Key Relationships to Build

| Who | Why | When to Meet |
|-----|-----|--------------|
| Your manager (Director/VP) | Strategy, executive context | Weekly |
| Direct reports (managers) | Development, alignment | Weekly each |
| Skip-levels (ICs) | Pulse check, culture | Monthly rotation |
| Product leadership | Partnership, strategy | Weekly |
| Peer executives | Collaboration, alignment | First month, ongoing |
| Executive team | Organizational context | As appropriate |

---

## Management Team Assessment

### For Each Manager (M1/M2)
- **Performance:** Meeting expectations for their level?
- **Potential:** Can they grow to next level?
- **Development needs:** What are they working on?
- **Team health:** How is their team doing?
- **Support needed:** What do they need from you?

### Leadership Team Health
- Trust and psychological safety
- Decision-making effectiveness
- Communication patterns
- Collaboration vs. silos
- Culture alignment

---

## Organizational Health Assessment

### Delivery
- Execution consistency
- Quality and reliability
- Velocity and predictability
- Technical debt situation

### People
- Engagement and morale
- Attrition and retention
- Talent pipeline
- Development culture

### Culture
- Values alignment
- Psychological safety
- Innovation vs. execution balance
- Cross-team collaboration

---

## Common M3 Onboarding Challenges

| Challenge | Solution |
|-----------|----------|
| Weak managers on team | Assess honestly; develop or transition; don't tolerate too long |
| Organizational silos | Build cross-team relationships; model collaboration; address structurally |
| Broken executive relationships | Invest in repair; understand history; demonstrate partnership |
| Unclear organizational direction | Work with your manager to clarify; create clarity for your org |
| Legacy of poor decisions | Acknowledge; clean up thoughtfully; don't blame predecessors |

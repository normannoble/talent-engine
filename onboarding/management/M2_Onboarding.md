# 30-60-90 Day Onboarding: Senior Engineering Manager (M2)

## Overview

**Level:** M2 â€” Senior Engineering Manager
**Focus:** Assessing team health, establishing independent operation, driving improvements
**Manager's Role:** Strategic partner, context provider, minimal oversight needed

M2s are experienced managers who operate independently. The goal is to quickly assess the team, establish effective management, and begin driving improvements.

---

## Days 1-30: Learn

### Theme: Assess the Landscape

**Primary Objectives:**
- Deeply understand team health, capabilities, and challenges
- Build relationships with team and stakeholders
- Assess current state of delivery and process
- Identify highest-priority improvements

### Week 1: Comprehensive Orientation

| Day | Focus | Activities |
|-----|-------|------------|
| 1 | Welcome | Manager intro, org context, strategic priorities |
| 2 | Team Deep-Dive | Individual meetings with each report (45-60 min) |
| 3 | Delivery | Current projects, roadmap, delivery health |
| 4 | Process | Team processes, tools, ceremonies, pain points |
| 5 | Stakeholders | PM, design, cross-functional partners, peer managers |

**Key Meetings to Schedule:**
- [ ] 1:1 with your manager (weekly)
- [ ] Extended 1:1 with each direct report
- [ ] Product manager (detailed working session)
- [ ] Key stakeholders and partners
- [ ] Peer managers in the organization
- [ ] Previous manager (if available)
- [ ] Skip-level (your manager's manager)

### Weeks 2-4: Deep Assessment

**Learning Goals:**
- [ ] Clear assessment of each team member's performance and potential
- [ ] Understanding of team dynamics and culture
- [ ] View of delivery health (velocity, quality, reliability)
- [ ] Map of stakeholder relationships and quality
- [ ] Identified improvements with highest leverage

**Activities:**
- Career conversations with each report
- Review historical performance data
- Assess technical health and debt
- Understand processes and their effectiveness
- Document assessment and initial recommendations

**Assessment Milestones:**
- [ ] Performance profile for each team member
- [ ] Team health assessment complete
- [ ] Delivery analysis done
- [ ] Process pain points identified
- [ ] Stakeholder relationships mapped

### 30-Day Check-in

**Your Manager Discussion Points:**
- What's your assessment of the team?
- What's working well?
- What are the highest-priority improvements?
- Who are your strongest performers? Concerns?
- What support do you need?

**Success Indicators:**
- [ ] Comprehensive team assessment complete
- [ ] Key relationships established
- [ ] Improvement priorities clear
- [ ] Operating with appropriate independence
- [ ] Team comfortable with new leadership

## Days 31-60: Contribute

### Theme: Drive Improvement

**Primary Objectives:**
- Begin implementing improvements
- Operate independently on all standard management
- Address performance or development needs
- Establish your leadership style with the team

### Weeks 5-6: Action Mode

**Activities:**
- Implement first high-leverage improvements
- Have performance conversations as needed
- Drive delivery and quality improvements
- Strengthen cross-functional partnerships
- Begin developing high-potential team members

**Driving Change:**
- [ ] First improvements implemented
- [ ] Tough conversations had
- [ ] Delivery cadence improving
- [ ] Stakeholder relationships strengthened
- [ ] Team responding to your leadership

### Weeks 7-8: Building Momentum

**Activities:**
- Continue improvement agenda
- Handle complex situations independently
- Drive hiring or team composition changes if needed
- Build peer relationships across organization
- Establish sustainable operating rhythm

**Operating Independently:**
- [ ] Standard situations handled without escalation
- [ ] Team delivery consistent
- [ ] Improvements taking hold
- [ ] Strong stakeholder relationships
- [ ] Finding your rhythm

### 60-Day Check-in

**Your Manager Discussion Points:**
- What improvements have you made?
- How is the team responding?
- What challenges are you navigating?
- How are cross-functional relationships?
- What's your plan for the next 30 days?

**Success Indicators:**
- [ ] Meaningful improvements delivered
- [ ] Team healthy and productive
- [ ] Operating with full independence
- [ ] Stakeholder confidence high
- [ ] Clear on continued priorities

## Team Assessment Framework

### Individual Assessment
For each team member, assess:
- Current performance level (high/solid/low)
- Trajectory (improving/stable/declining)
- Potential (high/moderate/limited)
- Flight risk (high/medium/low)
- Development priorities

### Team Health Assessment
- Morale and engagement
- Psychological safety
- Trust in leadership
- Team dynamics
- Attrition risk

### Delivery Assessment
- Velocity and predictability
- Quality (bugs, incidents)
- Technical debt situation
- Process effectiveness
- Stakeholder satisfaction

---

## Common M2 Onboarding Challenges

| Challenge | Solution |
|-----------|----------|
| Inherited underperformers | Assess fairly; act decisively but humanely |
| Broken stakeholder relationships | Invest in repair; understand history; rebuild trust |
| Team resistant to change | Build trust first; explain why; involve them |
| Unclear priorities | Clarify with your manager and PM; make decisions |
| Process is a mess | Pick one or two things to fix; don't overhaul everything |

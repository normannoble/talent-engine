# 30-60-90 Day Onboarding: Engineering Manager (M1)

## Overview

**Level:** M1 — Engineering Manager
**Focus:** Learning the team, building trust, establishing management fundamentals
**Manager's Role:** Coach, guide, support system for first-time or newer manager

M1s are often new to management or still developing core skills. The goal is to build team relationships, establish basic rhythms, and learn to lead through others.

---

## Days 1-30: Learn

### Theme: Know Your Team

**Primary Objectives:**
- Build individual relationships with every team member
- Understand team context, history, and dynamics
- Learn current projects, processes, and challenges
- Establish foundational management rhythms

### Week 1: Team Orientation

| Day | Focus | Activities |
|-----|-------|------------|
| 1 | Welcome | Manager intro, team overview, org context |
| 2 | Team | Meet each team member individually (30 min each) |
| 3 | Work | Current projects, roadmap, technical landscape |
| 4 | Process | Team ceremonies, tools, communication norms |
| 5 | Stakeholders | Meet PM, design, key cross-functional partners |

**Key Meetings to Schedule:**
- [ ] 1:1 with your manager (weekly)
- [ ] 1:1 with each direct report (schedule weekly cadence)
- [ ] Product manager
- [ ] Designer (if applicable)
- [ ] Engineering peers (other M1s/M2s)
- [ ] Previous manager of team (if available)

### Weeks 2-4: Deep Relationship Building

**Learning Goals:**
- [ ] Know each team member: strengths, goals, concerns, working style
- [ ] Understand team dynamics and relationships
- [ ] Know current project status and upcoming work
- [ ] Understand team's technical domain (enough to provide guidance)
- [ ] Identify immediate concerns or issues

**Activities:**
- Extended 1:1s with each report (60+ min career conversations)
- Observe team ceremonies (don't change anything yet)
- Learn the codebase at a high level
- Understand performance history of each team member
- Document observations and questions

**Relationship Milestones:**
- [ ] 1:1s established with every direct report
- [ ] Career goals documented for each person
- [ ] Trust beginning to form
- [ ] Team comfortable coming to you with questions
- [ ] Cross-functional partners connected

### 30-Day Check-in

**Your Manager Discussion Points:**
- What have you learned about each team member?
- What's the team's current state (health, morale, delivery)?
- What immediate concerns have you identified?
- What support do you need?
- How are you feeling about the transition to management?

**Success Indicators:**
- [ ] Relationships established with all reports
- [ ] Team context well understood
- [ ] Basic rhythms in place (1:1s, team meetings)
- [ ] No major surprises or fires
- [ ] Team feels supported

## Days 31-60: Contribute

### Theme: Establish Your Leadership

**Primary Objectives:**
- Run effective 1:1s and team meetings
- Begin coaching and developing team members
- Handle day-to-day management with increasing confidence
- Start making your own decisions

### Weeks 5-6: Active Management

**Activities:**
- Own 1:1s fully (agenda, action items, follow-up)
- Facilitate team meetings effectively
- Start giving feedback (both positive and developmental)
- Handle first performance conversations
- Begin delegating and empowering

**Management Fundamentals:**
- [ ] 1:1s are valuable for both parties
- [ ] Feedback being given and received
- [ ] Team meetings productive
- [ ] Basic blockers being removed
- [ ] Making decisions with appropriate autonomy

### Weeks 7-8: Growing Confidence

**Activities:**
- Navigate first challenging situations
- Coach team members on specific issues
- Represent team in cross-functional discussions
- Start thinking about team improvements
- Build peer relationships with other managers

**Growing Into Role:**
- [ ] Handling challenges without always escalating
- [ ] Team members developing under your guidance
- [ ] Cross-functional partnerships productive
- [ ] Starting to have opinions on improvements
- [ ] Finding your management style

### 60-Day Check-in

**Your Manager Discussion Points:**
- How are 1:1s and team meetings going?
- What challenging situations have you navigated?
- How is your confidence developing?
- What's working in your management approach?
- What do you find most difficult?

**Success Indicators:**
- [ ] Management rhythms effective
- [ ] Team trusts you
- [ ] Feedback flowing in both directions
- [ ] Handling standard situations
- [ ] Clear on where you need support

---

## Days 61-90: Own

### Theme: Operate as Manager

**Primary Objectives:**
- Manage independently for standard situations
- Develop your team actively
- Own team delivery and health
- Establish as the team's leader

### Weeks 9-10: Independent Management

**Activities:**
- Own team delivery and commitments
- Handle performance and development conversations
- Drive improvements you've identified
- Mentor junior team members
- Operate without close oversight

**M1 Responsibilities Met:**
- [ ] Team delivering on commitments
- [ ] Team members growing
- [ ] Feedback culture established
- [ ] Basic process working well
- [ ] You're the team's leader

### Weeks 11-12: Establishing Trajectory

**Activities:**
- Set goals for next quarter
- Plan team development activities
- Identify process improvements
- Build on early wins
- Transition from "new manager" mindset

**Looking Ahead:**
- [ ] Development plan for the team
- [ ] Personal growth plan as manager
- [ ] Clear on M1 expectations
- [ ] Understand path to M2
- [ ] Sustainable management rhythm

### 90-Day Review

**Your Manager Discussion Points:**
- Overall assessment of management transition
- Team health and delivery evaluation
- Your growth as a manager
- Path forward
- Recognition and development areas

**Success Indicators:**
- [ ] Operating as team's manager
- [ ] Team healthy and productive
- [ ] Your management skills developing
- [ ] Handling responsibilities independently
- [ ] Clear trajectory for growth

## Key Relationships to Build

| Who | Why | When to Meet |
|-----|-----|--------------|
| Your manager | Coaching, support, alignment | Weekly |
| Direct reports | Trust, development, delivery | Weekly each |
| Product manager | Planning, priorities, collaboration | Weekly |
| Peer managers | Support, learning, collaboration | First month, ongoing |
| Skip-level (your manager's manager) | Context, visibility | First month |

---

## Management Fundamentals Checklist

### 1:1s
- [ ] Scheduled weekly with each report
- [ ] Agenda is their agenda (not status updates)
- [ ] Taking notes and following up
- [ ] Discussing career, not just work
- [ ] Creating safe space for honesty

### Feedback
- [ ] Giving specific, timely positive feedback
- [ ] Giving constructive feedback when needed
- [ ] Receiving feedback openly
- [ ] Creating feedback culture on team

### Team Meetings
- [ ] Clear purpose and agenda
- [ ] Everyone participating
- [ ] Action items captured
- [ ] Not just status reporting

### Delegation
- [ ] Clear ownership assigned
- [ ] Context provided
- [ ] Not micromanaging
- [ ] Following up appropriately

---

## Common M1 Onboarding Challenges

| Challenge | Solution |
|-----------|----------|
| Team doesn't trust new manager | Be patient; listen more than talk; honor commitments |
| Struggling to give feedback | Start with positive; be specific; practice with your manager |
| Can't let go of IC work | Schedule management time; trust your team; resist coding |
| Overwhelmed by people issues | Lean on your manager; focus on one thing at a time |
| Imposter syndrome | Normal for new managers; seek support; learn continuously |

## Manager's Manager Checklist

### Pre-Arrival
- [ ] Team briefed on new manager
- [ ] 1:1 schedule template prepared
- [ ] Context document on each team member
- [ ] Key stakeholder intros scheduled
- [ ] First-week calendar set

### Week 1
- [ ] Team introductions complete
- [ ] Context shared on team members
- [ ] 1:1 cadence established
- [ ] Cross-functional intros done
- [ ] Daily/frequent check-ins happening

### Ongoing
- [ ] Supporting through challenges
- [ ] Not rescuing—coaching
- [ ] Providing feedback on management
- [ ] Celebrating wins
- [ ] Watching for burnout or overwhelm

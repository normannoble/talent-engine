# 30-60-90 Day Onboarding: Chief Technology Officer (E3)

## Overview

**Level:** E3 — Chief Technology Officer
**Focus:** Understanding company deeply, board relationships, technology vision
**Manager's Role:** CEO partnership, strategic alignment, organizational authority

The CTO owns ultimate accountability for technology. The goal is to quickly understand the company, build key relationships, and begin shaping long-term technology direction.

---

## Days 1-30: Learn

### Theme: Company-Wide Understanding

**Primary Objectives:**
- Deeply understand company strategy, business model, and competitive landscape
- Build relationships with CEO, board, and executive team
- Assess technology organization and capability
- Understand investor and customer context

### Week 1: C-Level Immersion

| Day | Focus | Activities |
|-----|-------|------------|
| 1 | Welcome | CEO deep-dive on company strategy, priorities, challenges |
| 2 | Business | Business model, financials, key metrics, competitive landscape |
| 3 | Technology | Technology organization, strategy, major initiatives |
| 4 | Executives | Executive team introductions and 1:1s |
| 5 | Board | Board context, key board members, governance |

**Key Meetings to Schedule:**
- [ ] CEO 1:1 (multiple times per week initially)
- [ ] Extended 1:1 with each executive team member
- [ ] Board members (individual introductions)
- [ ] E1s and E2s in technology organization
- [ ] Key customers (strategic relationships)
- [ ] Key investors (if appropriate)
- [ ] Major technology partners

### Weeks 2-4: Comprehensive Assessment

**Learning Goals:**
- [ ] Deep understanding of company strategy and how technology enables it
- [ ] Assessment of technology organization health and capability
- [ ] Understanding of major technical systems and their health
- [ ] Map of key relationships—board, investors, customers, partners
- [ ] View of technology risks and opportunities

**Activities:**
- Executive team integration and relationship building
- Technology organization deep-dive with E1s/E2s
- Customer and partner meetings
- Board member relationship building
- Major systems and architecture review
- Document comprehensive assessment

**Assessment Milestones:**
- [ ] Company strategy deeply understood
- [ ] Technology organization assessed
- [ ] Executive relationships established
- [ ] Board relationships forming
- [ ] Strategic technology view developing

### 30-Day Check-in

**CEO Discussion Points:**
- What's your assessment of the technology organization?
- What technology opportunities do you see?
- What are the biggest technology risks?
- How can we partner most effectively?
- What's your 60-day plan?

**Success Indicators:**
- [ ] Company strategy internalized
- [ ] Technology organization assessed
- [ ] Executive relationships solid
- [ ] Board relationships forming
- [ ] Strategic clarity emerging

- Build customer and partner relationships

**C-Level Leadership:**
- [ ] Technology strategy gaining traction

## Key Relationships to Build

| Who | Why | When to Meet |
|-----|-----|--------------|
| CEO | Primary partnership | Multiple times weekly |
| Executive team | Peer leadership | Weekly with each |
| Board members | Governance, credibility | First month, quarterly |
| E1s/E2s | Leadership, development | Weekly with directs |
| Key investors | Confidence, support | As appropriate |
| Key customers | Strategic relationships | First month, ongoing |
| Industry peers | External perspective, reputation | Ongoing |

---

## CEO Partnership Model

### What Makes It Work
- Aligned vision for technology's role
- Clear communication and no surprises
- Mutual trust and respect
- Complementary perspectives
- Shared accountability for outcomes

### Key Questions to Align On
- What role does technology play in our strategy?
- How do we make major technology decisions?
- What board involvement do you expect from me?
- How do we handle technology crises together?
- What does success look like in year one?

---

## Board Relationship Building

### Initial Approach
- Individual introductions with each board member
- Understand their perspective and concerns
- Learn their communication preferences
- Build credibility through competence and transparency

### Ongoing Board Management
- Regular updates on technology strategy and execution
- Proactive communication on risks
- Clear, executive-level presentations
- Build confidence over time
- Handle questions with composure

---

## Company-Wide Assessment Framework

### Business Assessment
- Business model strength
- Competitive position
- Market opportunities
- Customer dynamics
- Financial health

### Technology Assessment
- Technology strategy clarity
- Execution capability
- Technical health and debt
- Security and risk posture
- Innovation capacity

### Organizational Assessment
- Leadership team strength
- Talent depth
- Culture and engagement
- Organizational effectiveness
- Development pipeline

### External Assessment
- Market position
- Customer relationships
- Partner ecosystem
- Investor confidence
- Industry reputation

---

## Common CTO Onboarding Challenges

| Challenge | Solution |
|-----------|----------|
| Unclear CEO partnership | Discuss explicitly; define early; over-communicate |
| Board expectations unknown | Learn governance rhythm; build relationships; ask for feedback |
| Technology organization in poor shape | Honest assessment; prioritize ruthlessly; set realistic expectations |
| Executive team dynamics complex | Observe first; build relationships; address issues thoughtfully |
| External pressure high | Manage expectations; communicate clearly; deliver results |

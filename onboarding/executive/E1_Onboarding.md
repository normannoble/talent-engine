# 30-60-90 Day Onboarding: Director of Engineering (E1)

## Overview

**Level:** E1 — Director of Engineering
**Focus:** Understanding domain, building executive relationships, establishing business ownership
**Manager's Role:** Executive partner, strategic alignment, organizational authority

E1s own technology domains with full business accountability. The goal is to quickly assess the domain, build executive partnerships, and establish strategic ownership.

---

## Days 1-30: Learn

### Theme: Domain & Executive Assessment

**Primary Objectives:**
- Understand domain comprehensively (technology, teams, business outcomes)
- Build relationships with executive team and key stakeholders
- Assess organizational capability and talent
- Map business context and competitive landscape

### Week 1: Executive Immersion

| Day | Focus | Activities |
|-----|-------|------------|
| 1 | Welcome | CEO/CTO intro, company strategy, executive priorities |
| 2 | Domain | Deep-dive on your domain—teams, systems, business outcomes |
| 3 | Leadership | Meet M3s and senior managers in your domain |
| 4 | Business | Business model, metrics, competitive landscape |
| 5 | Executives | Meet peer executives (Product, Design, Finance, etc.) |

**Key Meetings to Schedule:**
- [ ] 1:1 with CTO/VP (weekly)
- [ ] Extended 1:1s with direct reports (M3s)
- [ ] CEO or business leadership
- [ ] Peer executives across functions
- [ ] Key customers (if appropriate)
- [ ] Board members (if appropriate)
- [ ] Skip-level meetings across domain

### Weeks 2-4: Comprehensive Assessment

**Learning Goals:**
- [ ] Deep understanding of domain business outcomes and metrics
- [ ] Assessment of leadership team (M3s) capability
- [ ] View of organizational health across domain
- [ ] Understanding of technical strategy and execution capability
- [ ] Map of executive relationships and dynamics

**Activities:**
- Leadership assessment for each M3
- Skip-level meetings across the organization
- Business metric deep-dives
- Technology strategy review
- Executive relationship building
- Document domain assessment

**Assessment Milestones:**
- [ ] Domain business health understood
- [ ] Leadership team assessed
- [ ] Organizational capability mapped
- [ ] Executive relationships forming
- [ ] Strategic opportunities identified

### 30-Day Check-in

**Your Manager Discussion Points:**
- What's your assessment of the domain?
- How is the leadership team?
- What are the biggest opportunities and risks?
- How are executive relationships developing?
- What's your 60-day plan?

**Success Indicators:**
- [ ] Comprehensive domain assessment complete
- [ ] M3 relationships established
- [ ] Executive partnerships forming
- [ ] Business context deeply understood
- [ ] Strategic clarity emerging

## Key Relationships to Build

| Who | Why | When to Meet |
|-----|-----|--------------|
| CTO/VP Eng | Direct partnership, alignment | Weekly |
| M3s (direct reports) | Leadership, development | Weekly each |
| CEO | Company context, visibility | First month, monthly |
| Peer executives | Cross-functional partnership | Weekly with key partners |
| Board (if applicable) | Governance, credibility | As appropriate |
| Skip-levels | Pulse check, culture | Monthly rotation |

---

## Domain Assessment Framework

### Business Assessment
- Revenue/cost impact of domain
- Key metrics and performance
- Customer impact
- Competitive position
- Investment effectiveness

### Organizational Assessment
- Leadership team (M3s) capability
- Talent depth and bench strength
- Culture and engagement
- Delivery capability
- Technical health

### Strategic Assessment
- Alignment with company strategy
- Market trends and opportunities
- Technical differentiation
- Investment priorities
- Risk landscape

---

## Common E1 Onboarding Challenges

| Challenge | Solution |
|-----------|----------|
| Weak M3s in domain | Assess honestly; develop or transition; build bench |
| Broken executive relationships | Invest in repair; demonstrate partnership; deliver results |
| Unclear business metrics | Work to define; connect technology to outcomes |
| Domain in poor shape | Honest assessment; prioritize; set realistic expectations |
| Competing executive priorities | Build relationships; understand context; find alignment |

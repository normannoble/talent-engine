# 30-60-90 Day Onboarding: Senior Software Engineer (IC3)

## Overview

**Level:** IC3 â€” Senior Software Engineer
**Focus:** Establishing ownership, understanding systems, influencing technical direction
**Manager's Role:** Context provider, strategic guidance, appropriate autonomy

IC3s are experienced engineers expected to own outcomes and influence decisions. The goal is to quickly establish domain ownership and begin shaping technical direction.

---

## Days 1-30: Learn

### Theme: Map the Territory

**Primary Objectives:**
- Deeply understand assigned systems and domain
- Build relationships across the team and organization
- Identify opportunities for impact
- Understand team dynamics and technical landscape

### Week 1: Strategic Orientation

| Day | Focus | Activities |
|-----|-------|------------|
| 1 | Welcome | Team intros, org context, strategic priorities |
| 2 | Product | Deep-dive on product area, user problems, business metrics |
| 3 | Architecture | System architecture, key services, data flows |
| 4 | Technical Debt | Current pain points, tech debt, improvement opportunities |
| 5 | Stakeholders | Meet PM, design, dependent teams, key engineers |

**Key Meetings to Schedule:**
- [ ] Manager 1:1 (weekly)
- [ ] 1:1 with each teammate
- [ ] Product manager deep-dive
- [ ] Staff/Principal engineers (if present)
- [ ] Key stakeholders from dependent teams
- [ ] Previous owner of your domain (if applicable)

### Weeks 2-4: Deep Immersion

**Learning Goals:**
- [ ] Expert-level understanding of your primary domain
- [ ] Clear view of system health, risks, and opportunities
- [ ] Understanding of team's technical strategy and roadmap
- [ ] Map of key stakeholders and decision-makers
- [ ] Initial assessment of where you can add most value

**Activities:**
- Own a meaningful project (demonstrate capability early)
- Review architecture decisions and RFCs
- Understand monitoring, alerting, and system health
- Identify areas needing investment
- Document findings and initial recommendations

**Technical Milestones:**
- [ ] Can operate independently in your domain
- [ ] First meaningful contribution shipped
- [ ] Understand the "why" behind architectural decisions
- [ ] Can debug complex issues in your systems
- [ ] Have initial views on improvements

### 30-Day Check-in

**Manager Discussion Points:**
- What's your assessment of the domain/systems?
- What opportunities have you identified?
- How are relationships developing?
- What context or support do you need?
- Initial feedback on contributions

**Success Indicators:**
- [ ] Clear mental model of domain and systems
- [ ] Key relationships established
- [ ] First contributions demonstrating IC3 capability
- [ ] Identified 2-3 high-value opportunities
- [ ] Operating with appropriate ownership

---

## Days 31-60: Contribute

### Theme: Drive Impact

**Primary Objectives:**
- Own outcomes for your domain
- Influence technical decisions
- Deliver significant work
- Begin mentoring and multiplying

### Weeks 5-6: Establishing Ownership

**Activities:**
- Take full ownership of assigned domain/systems
- Drive technical decisions in your area
- Lead a meaningful project or initiative
- Mentor more junior engineers
- Contribute to technical planning

**Demonstrating Ownership:**
- [ ] You're the "go-to" for your area
- [ ] Making architectural decisions with confidence
- [ ] Proactively addressing issues and risks
- [ ] Clear communication with stakeholders
- [ ] Quality bar maintained and improved

### Weeks 7-8: Expanding Influence

**Activities:**
- Start influencing beyond immediate domain
- Contribute to team-wide technical discussions
- Share knowledge through documentation or tech talks
- Build relationships with staff engineers
- Represent your area in cross-team discussions

**Growing Impact:**
- [ ] Technical influence extending beyond immediate work
- [ ] Junior engineers seeking your guidance
- [ ] Contributing to team standards and practices
- [ ] Voice in technical direction
- [ ] Recognized expertise in your area

### 60-Day Check-in

**Manager Discussion Points:**
- How is ownership feeling?
- What impact have you had so far?
- Where do you want to expand influence?
- How are mentoring relationships?
- Feedback on technical leadership

**Success Indicators:**
- [ ] Full ownership of domain established
- [ ] Meaningful technical decisions made
- [ ] Mentoring and multiplying effect visible
- [ ] Stakeholder relationships solid
- [ ] Contributing at IC3 level consistently

---

## Days 61-90: Own

### Theme: Solidify as Senior Engineer

**Primary Objectives:**
- Operate fully at IC3 level
- Shape technical direction for your area
- Demonstrate sustained impact
- Establish trajectory for continued growth

### Weeks 9-10: Full IC3 Operation

**Activities:**
- Own outcomes completely for your domain
- Drive improvements and initiatives
- Lead technical decisions confidently
- Mentor effectively
- Contribute to team strategy

**IC3 Expectations Met:**
- [ ] End-to-end ownership of systems/projects
- [ ] Technical decisions driving outcomes
- [ ] Multiplying through mentoring
- [ ] Strong stakeholder relationships
- [ ] Quality and reliability bar maintained

### Weeks 11-12: Setting Long-Term Trajectory

**Activities:**
- Document technical vision for your area
- Set goals for next quarter
- Discuss IC4 path if interested
- Solidify position and reputation
- Fully transition from "new hire" mindset

**Looking Ahead:**
- [ ] Clear vision for your domain
- [ ] Development plan established
- [ ] Relationships solidified
- [ ] Known as go-to senior in your area
- [ ] Positioned for continued impact

### 90-Day Review

**Manager Discussion Points:**
- Comprehensive onboarding assessment
- Technical ownership evaluation
- Influence and leadership observed
- Path forward (IC3 depth vs. IC4 breadth)
- Recognition and growth areas

**Success Indicators:**
- [ ] Operating fully at IC3 level
- [ ] Ownership clearly established
- [ ] Technical influence demonstrated
- [ ] Strong relationships and reputation
- [ ] Clear on continued growth path

## Key Relationships to Build

| Who | Why | When to Meet |
|-----|-----|--------------|
| Direct manager | Alignment, feedback, growth | Weekly |
| Product manager | Domain strategy, priorities | Weekly or biweekly |
| Staff engineers | Technical mentorship, influence | First month, ongoing |
| Junior engineers | Mentoring, multiplying | Ongoing |
| Cross-team tech leads | Dependencies, collaboration | First month, as needed |
| Stakeholders | Domain expertise, alignment | First month, ongoing |

---

## Common IC3 Onboarding Challenges

| Challenge | Solution |
|-----------|----------|
| Not given enough ownership | Advocate for scope; demonstrate readiness; clear handoffs |
| Overwhelmed by system complexity | Prioritize understanding; ask for context; document learnings |
| Stepping on toes | Navigate carefully; build relationships; understand history |
| Not influencing effectively | Build credibility first; understand decision-making culture |
| Mentoring not landing | Start small; be available; focus on high-leverage moments |

## Manager Checklist

### Pre-Arrival
- [ ] Domain/ownership area identified
- [ ] Handoff plan from previous owner
- [ ] Key stakeholder meetings scheduled
- [ ] Initial project scoped
- [ ] Team expectations set

### Week 1
- [ ] Architecture deep-dive complete
- [ ] Stakeholder introductions done
- [ ] Domain context transferred
- [ ] First project kicked off
- [ ] Regular cadence established

### Ongoing
- [ ] Ownership transfer progressing
- [ ] Influence opportunities provided
- [ ] Mentoring relationships forming
- [ ] Technical leadership developing
- [ ] Course corrections made as needed

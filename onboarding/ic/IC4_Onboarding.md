# 30-60-90 Day Onboarding: Staff Software Engineer (IC4)

## Overview

**Level:** IC4 â€” Staff Software Engineer
**Focus:** Assessing landscape, identifying high-impact opportunities, building cross-team influence
**Manager's Role:** Strategic partner, door-opener, organizational context

IC4s drive organizational-level technical impact. The goal is to quickly understand the broader landscape and identify where strategic technical leadership is most needed.

---

## Days 1-30: Learn

### Theme: Strategic Assessment

**Primary Objectives:**
- Understand organizational technical landscape
- Build relationships across engineering leadership
- Identify high-impact opportunities
- Understand company strategy and how technology enables it

### Week 1: Executive Orientation

| Day | Focus | Activities |
|-----|-------|------------|
| 1 | Welcome | Leadership intros, org structure, company strategy |
| 2 | Architecture | System-wide architecture, major platforms, tech stack evolution |
| 3 | Strategy | Engineering strategy, roadmap, major initiatives |
| 4 | Challenges | Key technical challenges, debt, organizational pain points |
| 5 | Relationships | Meet engineering directors, staff+ engineers, key stakeholders |

**Key Meetings to Schedule:**
- [ ] Manager 1:1 (weekly)
- [ ] VP/Director of Engineering
- [ ] Other Staff/Principal engineers
- [ ] Engineering managers across teams
- [ ] Product leadership
- [ ] Key technical leads in different areas

### Weeks 2-4: Landscape Mapping

**Learning Goals:**
- [ ] Clear view of organizational technical strategy
- [ ] Understanding of major systems and their health
- [ ] Map of where technical leadership is needed
- [ ] Relationships with key technical and business leaders
- [ ] Initial hypotheses on highest-impact opportunities

**Activities:**
- Conduct listening tour across engineering
- Review major architecture decisions and RFCs
- Understand ongoing major initiatives
- Identify gaps in technical leadership
- Assess team capabilities and technical depth
- Document observations and opportunities

**Strategic Milestones:**
- [ ] Can articulate organizational technical strategy
- [ ] Understand key systems and their interconnections
- [ ] Know the major technical challenges and opportunities
- [ ] Have relationships with key leaders
- [ ] Initial view on where to focus

### 30-Day Check-in

**Manager Discussion Points:**
- What's your assessment of our technical landscape?
- What high-impact opportunities do you see?
- Where is technical leadership most needed?
- What relationships are you building?
- What context do you still need?

**Success Indicators:**
- [ ] Comprehensive view of technical org
- [ ] Key relationships established
- [ ] 2-3 high-impact opportunities identified
- [ ] Clear on organizational context and dynamics
- [ ] Beginning to form strategic point of view

---

## Days 31-60: Contribute

### Theme: Strategic Initiative

**Primary Objectives:**
- Drive one or more strategic initiatives
- Build cross-team alignment and influence
- Establish technical authority
- Begin shaping organizational technical direction

### Weeks 5-6: Launching Impact

**Activities:**
- Lead a strategic technical initiative
- Build coalition for cross-team work
- Write RFC or strategy document for major initiative
- Mentor senior engineers toward Staff capability
- Participate in engineering leadership discussions

**Establishing Leadership:**
- [ ] Driving work that spans teams
- [ ] Building consensus across stakeholders
- [ ] Making strategic technical decisions
- [ ] Contributing to engineering strategy
- [ ] Recognized as technical leader

### Weeks 7-8: Building Momentum

**Activities:**
- Advance strategic initiative significantly
- Influence technical standards and practices broadly
- Develop senior engineers
- Contribute to hiring and talent strategy
- Shape technical culture

**Cross-Team Impact:**
- [ ] Multiple teams influenced by your work
- [ ] Technical direction being shaped
- [ ] Senior engineers growing under your guidance
- [ ] Organizational practices improving
- [ ] Voice in engineering leadership

### 60-Day Check-in

**Manager Discussion Points:**
- How is your strategic initiative progressing?
- What cross-team influence are you building?
- How is mentoring going?
- What obstacles are you facing?
- Feedback on technical leadership

**Success Indicators:**
- [ ] Strategic initiative making progress
- [ ] Cross-team relationships productive
- [ ] Influence extending across organization
- [ ] Mentoring relationships established
- [ ] Operating at IC4 scope

---

## Days 61-90: Own

### Theme: Establish as Organizational Technical Leader

**Primary Objectives:**
- Deliver on strategic initiative
- Solidify organizational influence
- Demonstrate IC4-level sustained impact
- Set long-term technical direction

### Weeks 9-10: Strategic Delivery

**Activities:**
- Ship or significantly advance strategic initiative
- Document technical strategy for your areas
- Establish ongoing influence mechanisms
- Develop multiple senior engineers
- Represent engineering perspective cross-functionally

**IC4 Impact Demonstrated:**
- [ ] Organization-level outcomes delivered
- [ ] Technical direction being set
- [ ] Multiple teams benefiting from your leadership
- [ ] Senior engineers growing to Staff trajectory
- [ ] Strategic voice in engineering

### Weeks 11-12: Long-Term Positioning

**Activities:**
- Set vision for continued technical leadership
- Identify next strategic opportunities
- Solidify position in engineering leadership
- Plan for sustained organizational impact
- Fully transition to "established leader" mindset

**Looking Ahead:**
- [ ] Long-term technical vision articulated
- [ ] Multiple strategic threads identified
- [ ] Positioned as go-to organizational technical leader
- [ ] Development plan for IC5 path (if interested)
- [ ] Sustainable operating rhythm established

### 90-Day Review

**Manager Discussion Points:**
- Comprehensive assessment of impact
- Strategic initiative outcomes
- Cross-team influence evaluation
- Path forward (IC4 depth vs. IC5)
- Recognition and growth areas

**Success Indicators:**
- [ ] Operating at IC4 scope consistently
- [ ] Organizational technical impact demonstrated
- [ ] Cross-team influence established
- [ ] Senior engineers being developed
- [ ] Clear long-term strategic role

## Key Relationships to Build

| Who | Why | When to Meet |
|-----|-----|--------------|
| Direct manager | Alignment, organizational context | Weekly |
| VP/Director Eng | Strategic alignment, sponsorship | First month, monthly |
| Other Staff+ engineers | Peer collaboration, alignment | First month, ongoing |
| Engineering managers | Team context, influence | First month, as needed |
| Product leadership | Strategic alignment | First month, ongoing |
| Senior engineers | Mentoring, developing | Ongoing |

---

## Common IC4 Onboarding Challenges

| Challenge | Solution |
|-----------|----------|
| Unclear on where to focus | Use first 30 days intentionally; get manager input on priorities |
| Difficulty building cross-team influence | Invest in relationships; find shared problems; build credibility |
| Stepping on existing leadership | Navigate carefully; add to, don't compete; align with existing Staff |
| Strategic work not scoped | Work with manager to define; use RFC process; create clarity |
| Not enough organizational context | Ask for it; seek out history; build relationships |

- [ ] Cross-team relationships developing

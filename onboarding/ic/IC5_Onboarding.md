# 30-60-90 Day Onboarding: Distinguished Software Engineer (IC5)

## Overview

**Level:** IC5 â€” Distinguished Software Engineer
**Focus:** Understanding company strategy, shaping technical vision, building executive relationships
**Manager's Role:** Executive partner, strategic alignment, organizational navigation

IC5s are company-level technical leaders. The goal is to quickly understand the company's strategic context and begin shaping long-term technical direction.

---

## Days 1-30: Learn

### Theme: Company-Level Understanding

**Primary Objectives:**
- Understand company strategy and competitive landscape
- Build relationships with executive leadership
- Assess technology organization comprehensively
- Identify company-level technical opportunities

### Week 1: Executive Immersion

| Day | Focus | Activities |
|-----|-------|------------|
| 1 | Welcome | Executive intros, company strategy briefing |
| 2 | Business | Business model, competitive landscape, market position |
| 3 | Technology | Technology strategy, major platforms, technical vision |
| 4 | Organization | Engineering organization, key leaders, talent landscape |
| 5 | Relationships | CTO, VPs, other Distinguished/Principal engineers |

**Key Meetings to Schedule:**
- [ ] CTO / VP Engineering (regular cadence)
- [ ] Executive team members
- [ ] Other IC5/Principal engineers
- [ ] Engineering Directors
- [ ] Product and business leadership
- [ ] Key technical leaders across org

### Weeks 2-4: Strategic Assessment

**Learning Goals:**
- [ ] Deep understanding of company strategy and how technology enables it
- [ ] Comprehensive view of technology organization strengths and gaps
- [ ] Map of key technical and business leaders
- [ ] Understanding of industry trends relevant to company
- [ ] Initial hypotheses on company-level technical opportunities

**Activities:**
- Executive listening tour across company
- Deep-dive on major technical systems and platforms
- Review technology strategy and long-term roadmap
- Assess engineering talent and organizational capability
- Document strategic observations and opportunities
- Connect with industry peers and maintain external perspective

**Strategic Milestones:**
- [ ] Can articulate company strategy and technology's role
- [ ] Understand major technical bets and their rationale
- [ ] Clear view of organizational capability and gaps
- [ ] Executive relationships forming
- [ ] Initial strategic technical perspective developing

### 30-Day Check-in

**Manager Discussion Points:**
- What's your assessment of our technology strategy?
- What company-level opportunities do you see?
- How are executive relationships developing?
- What unique perspective are you bringing?
- What additional context do you need?

**Success Indicators:**
- [ ] Comprehensive company-level understanding
- [ ] Executive relationships established
- [ ] Strategic opportunities identified
- [ ] External perspective being applied
- [ ] Beginning to shape technical vision

---

## Days 31-60: Contribute

### Theme: Strategic Leadership

**Primary Objectives:**
- Begin shaping company-level technical direction
- Drive or sponsor major strategic initiatives
- Build cross-organizational influence
- Develop Staff engineers toward Distinguished capability

### Weeks 5-6: Vision Formation

**Activities:**
- Draft technical vision or strategy document
- Sponsor or lead major technical initiative
- Contribute to executive-level technology discussions
- Mentor Staff engineers on strategic leadership
- Represent company technology externally (if appropriate)

**Executive Contribution:**
- [ ] Contributing to company-level strategy
- [ ] Shaping major technical decisions
- [ ] Building executive partnerships
- [ ] Developing future technical leaders
- [ ] External presence building

### Weeks 7-8: Strategic Execution

**Activities:**
- Advance strategic technical initiative
- Influence resource allocation and investment
- Build coalition for long-term technical bets
- Contribute to board/investor materials (if applicable)
- Shape engineering culture at company level

**Company-Level Impact:**
- [ ] Technical vision gaining traction
- [ ] Major initiative making progress
- [ ] Executive team values technical perspective
- [ ] Staff engineers growing under guidance
- [ ] Company-level decisions being influenced

### 60-Day Check-in

**Manager Discussion Points:**
- How is the technical vision developing?
- What executive relationships are strongest?
- What strategic initiatives are you driving?
- How is external presence developing?
- Feedback on company-level contribution

**Success Indicators:**
- [ ] Technical vision articulated and shared
- [ ] Strategic initiative in progress
- [ ] Executive partnership established
- [ ] Staff engineers being developed
- [ ] Operating at IC5 scope

---

## Days 61-90: Own

### Theme: Establish as Company Technical Leader

**Primary Objectives:**
- Solidify technical vision and strategy
- Demonstrate company-level sustained impact
- Establish as key voice in company direction
- Set long-term technical trajectory for company

### Weeks 9-10: Strategic Impact

**Activities:**
- Ship or significantly advance major initiative
- Present technical vision to executive team
- Establish ongoing executive partnership cadence
- Build technical leadership community
- Represent company externally

**IC5 Impact Demonstrated:**
- [ ] Company-level outcomes influenced
- [ ] Technical vision guiding decisions
- [ ] Executive team relies on technical perspective
- [ ] Staff engineers on path to Distinguished
- [ ] External presence established

### Weeks 11-12: Long-Term Vision

**Activities:**
- Set 3-5 year technical vision
- Identify next major strategic opportunities
- Solidify position as company technical leader
- Establish sustainable operating rhythm
- Plan for lasting technical impact

**Looking Ahead:**
- [ ] Long-term technical vision documented
- [ ] Multiple strategic threads underway
- [ ] Positioned as key company technical leader
- [ ] Industry presence growing
- [ ] Sustainable leadership model established

### 90-Day Review

**Manager Discussion Points:**
- Comprehensive assessment of strategic impact
- Technical vision evaluation
- Executive partnership assessment
- Industry presence development
- Path forward and continued growth

**Success Indicators:**
- [ ] Operating at IC5 scope consistently
- [ ] Company-level technical impact demonstrated
- [ ] Executive partnerships strong
- [ ] Technical vision guiding organization
- [ ] Industry presence established

## Key Relationships to Build

| Who | Why | When to Meet |
|-----|-----|--------------|
| CTO / VP Eng | Primary partnership, strategic alignment | Weekly |
| Executive team | Company strategy, influence | First month, regular |
| Other IC5s | Peer collaboration, alignment | First month, ongoing |
| Engineering Directors | Organizational execution | First month, as needed |
| Staff engineers | Developing future leaders | Ongoing |
| Board (if applicable) | Company governance, credibility | As appropriate |
| Industry peers | External perspective, reputation | Ongoing |

---

## Common IC5 Onboarding Challenges

| Challenge | Solution |
|-----------|----------|
| Unclear on company strategy | Request executive briefings; build CTO relationship |
| Difficulty finding right scope | Work with CTO to identify highest-impact areas |
| Navigating executive dynamics | Observe before acting; build relationships carefully |
| Balancing internal/external | Be intentional about time; align with CTO on external activities |
| Developing Staff engineers | Be accessible; create structured mentoring; sponsor initiatives |
